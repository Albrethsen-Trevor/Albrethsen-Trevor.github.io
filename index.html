Past Experiences

Front-End Developer Intern

Jarvis Arms

I worked independently as the designer and the developer for Jarvis Arms using WordPress. The purpose of the website is to sell proprietary products created by Jarvis Arms, as it was an e-commerce website.

I worked closely with the owner of Jarvis Arms, communicating with him on a regular basis. I learned the value of communication and understanding throughout my internship. It's important that both parties are on the same page so progress and be made and flow smoothly.

The examples below (from left to right) showcase some of the design choices made for the Jarvis Arms website. The first image displays the home page. We went with this design style so end users can be fully aware what products are being sold by Jarvis Arms. We wanted to make sure they ended up on something that they were expecting when they entered the address or clicked on a link to the website.

The second image shows the use of active and hover effects through CSS. The theme originally lacked such a feature. It also adds more user interaction, which I think is important for a user to experience while on a website no matter how simple a website is.

The third image shows the responsiveness of the website. More and more people are using their phones or tablets to surf the web, so it is very important that websites look good on both computers and mobile devices.

Lastly, the fourth image displays a dropdown menu. The original dropdown menu was hard to differentiate from the menu and the white background color, so I made the background color black and the menu a gunmetal grey color. There's also a line to separate the menu from the Jarvis Arms banner.
